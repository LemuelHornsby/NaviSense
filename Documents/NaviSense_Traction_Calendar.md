## Appendix A · DIANA 2027 short-proposal outline (due 3 Jul 2026, 12:00 BST — submit 30 Jun)

Challenge: **Multidomain autonomy of uncrewed systems** (5 pages max). Suggested structure:

1. **Problem (½ p):** USV autonomy cannot be safely or affordably certified by sea trials alone; MASS Code (in effect 1 Jul 2026) + EBP demand repeatable simulation evidence; current tools are enterprise-priced (inaccessible to the innovation base) or research-grade (unvalidated physics).
2. **Solution (1½ p):** NaviSense — open-core simulation & evidence platform: CFD-validated maneuvering physics, photoreal georeferenced ports (Cesium/UE5), full synthetic sensor suite with auto-labels, scored COLREGS scenario packs, headless/CI operation, open Python/JSON control. Screenshot strip + architecture diagram.
3. **Dual-use logic (¾ p):** identical platform serves commercial MASS developers (COLREGS evidence) and defense USV T&E (scenario rehearsal, sensor-stack validation, operator familiarization); DIANA test-centre fit: integrates as the synthetic environment layer at maritime test centres.
4. **Maturity & roadmap (¾ p):** working closed loop + validation pipeline + demo film (link); 30-day public demo 11 Jul; community launch Aug; €100k phase: vessel/sensor model expansion, test-centre integration pilot, evidence-format hardening.
5. **Team & edge (½ p):** founder profile (naval-architecture CFD + full-stack sim engineering + AI-automated development velocity); advisory plan; why a solo deep-tech founder is fundable here (zero burn, working product, niche the primes ignore).
6. **Budget & milestones (½ p):** €100k / 6 months: 40% engineering, 25% validation campaign, 20% test-centre pilot, 15% compliance/dissemination; 3 measurable milestones.
